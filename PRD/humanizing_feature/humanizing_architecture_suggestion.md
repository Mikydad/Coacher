SidePal Vision

An AI companion that thinks, remembers, plans, and acts like a real human assistant who understands you.

Unlike traditional productivity apps, SidePal doesn't wait for commands. It proactively looks for opportunities to help you become the person you want to be.

1. The Assistant Philosophy

This is the core principle behind everything.

Traditional apps ask:

"What time should I remind you?"

SidePal asks:

"What are you trying to accomplish?"

Then it figures out the rest.

Examples:

You say: "I need to call my niece tomorrow."
SidePal chooses the best time.
SidePal notices if you're busy.
SidePal reschedules if necessary.
SidePal learns from your habits.

The user tells what they want.

SidePal decides how and when.

2. The Three-Brain Architecture
Brain 1 — Truth Engine

This is the source of truth.

It stores verified facts.

Examples:

Calendar
Tasks
Goals
Habits
Routines
Deadlines
Notes
Promises
User preferences
Long-term memory

It never guesses.

Brain 2 — Context Engine

This understands the current moment.

Examples:

Time
Current activity
Calendar status
Focus mode
Location (with permission)
Walking
Driving
Sleeping
Battery
Upcoming meetings

It answers:

"What is happening right now?"

Brain 3 — Reasoning Assistant (AI)

This is the personality.

It reasons using the facts from the other two systems.

It asks:

What should Mike do next?
Should I remind him now?
Should I wait?
Is he likely to complete this?
Should I encourage him?
Should I ask another question?

This is what makes SidePal feel human instead of rule-based.

3. Opportunity-Based Scheduling

Instead of scheduling by time...

Tomorrow 3:00 PM

...SidePal schedules by opportunity.

Example:

You say:

"Call my best friend tomorrow."

Instead of asking:

"What time?"

SidePal thinks:

Tomorrow is busy.
You usually call people while walking.
You're walking home at 5:30 PM.
That's a perfect opportunity.

Then it reminds you.

Every task has compatible contexts.

For example:

Doesn't work with:

Works with:

Waiting

Doesn't work with:

Driving

The AI understands which activities naturally fit together.

5. Opportunity Scoring

Every possible moment gets scored.

For example:

Walking home:

Walking is compatible

+ 20 minutes available

Calls usually happen here
Deadline is tomorrow

Final Score: 96

Working deeply:

Requires concentration

Final Score: 18

Highest score wins.

6. Memory & Learning

SidePal should continuously learn.

Examples:

It learns:

You call family while walking.
You ignore reminders after 9 PM.
You work best in the morning.
You procrastinate difficult conversations.
Friday afternoons are your admin time.

The AI becomes more personal over time.

7. Conversational AI

SidePal shouldn't feel like a chatbot.

It should feel like a conversation with someone who knows you.

Instead of:

User asks question.

AI answers.

It should think.

Example:

User:

"What should I work on tomorrow?"

AI reasons:

Calendar
Deadlines
Goals
Energy
Previous conversations
Habits
Promises

Then gives a thoughtful answer.

The AI isn't just explaining what an engine decided—it is reasoning over verified facts.

10. AI's Role

One of the biggest design decisions we made:

Don't let AI invent reality.

Instead:

Truth Engine provides facts.

Context Engine provides the present.

AI provides judgment.

This keeps the assistant reliable while still making it feel intelligent.

The Core Principles

If I had to write the design philosophy for SidePal in one place, it would be:

Understand the user's intent, not just their commands.
Reason over verified facts rather than guessing.
Schedule by opportunity, not by clock time.
Learn continuously from the user's behavior.
Be proactive, not reactive.
Use AI where judgment is needed and deterministic systems where accuracy matters.
Maintain one persistent assistant across text, voice, and every feature.
Always feel like a trusted person by the user's side, not a collection of productivity tools.
